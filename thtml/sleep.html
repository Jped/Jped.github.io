Conquering Sleep


It is obvious that sleep is vital for a successful day, but I am not positive that I completely buy the whole 7 hours for a successful day business.
I will tell you that the whole first semester of college I got about 7 hours of sleep every night, but I was always tired. Yet in the second semester
I got about 6.5 hours on average and was not as tired.
Sure, I did not exercise much, and it was the winter, but another big difference was that I was very conscious about the amount of hours I had.
If I did not get the 7 hours, I would tell myself that I was tired and that I need a nap even though I do not think I really needed it.
I think that really affected how I felt


Another vital part of sleep that I have still not mastered is waking up.
How I wake up reveals how good my discipline is.
My older brother once made the point that waking up is the first interaction you have with your desires. If you let your desires
and laziness to win, you snooze the alarm clock and go back to bed. If you win you jump right out of bed and get on with your day.
