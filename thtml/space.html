space

2017-05-01

I once heard one of my Rabbis, Rabbi Chamudot, comment that "a man has to know when to stop". He said this somewhat quietly, when he decided to
send us out of class early.

Its funny how this was such a small comment, but it has been in my head for a while recently.

The importance of taking a step back, or leaving space is vital.

Today specifically, I was working on a computer architecture project with my good friend and roommate. We are having this issue where we cant
successfully import a library. It such a stupid issue that should not exist, yet we have spent a good four hours on it...
Given that the atmosphere was pretty tense. He wants to try one thing, I want to try another and we butt heads.

The fatigue just makes it worse.
Honestly, I held myself pretty well, but I should have called it off at least an hour or so earlier... after a certain point there is no real progress, just arguments...
