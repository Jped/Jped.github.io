doing

2017-04-26

While in high school, I really thought I was a doer. I thought that was my qualifying trait. I do not think I am
idealising my high school career. I know this for two reasons.

  1. I remember in my stanford application they asked me for a few words that described me, I wrote doer
  2. I tried making a personal website a few year back and my title line was something like "Jonathan Pedoem. Doer. Thinker. ..."


Now, I do not know if I have changed. I do not know if I have become worse. I do feel that I am not in that position anymore, I feel as if I lost some of my mojo .
However, this can be due to a change of enviorment or something like that. or it can actually be true.

Yet, I do not think it matters that much. I just know now that I can be a better doer. I have this image in my head of what I want to achieve. I want to be able to go around
and put my mind on something and do it.
I know this is a huge thing that will be difficult, so I am patient. Recently, I have come more attuned to this patience thing. Patience, while simple on paper, is tough.

This thought it kinds of scary too. Recently (namly after last semester), I have become more fearful. I am scared to do things. Kinda sucks, but then again I know what I got to do
I just go to keep focused and stay patient till I get where I want to go.

I dont think doing is only an innate trait. I believe it is a muscle that starts breaking exponently once you stop using it.
To be a doer you go to do and do and do with no questions or excuses asked. Once there is a break in the doing you are done.
There is not easy way. Just got to do, starting today, starting now (ie with writing this).
